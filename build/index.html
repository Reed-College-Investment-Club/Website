About us

Founding father Benjamin Franklin famously stated that "an investment in knowledge pays the best interest." These words inform and shape the Stanford Investment Club's primary purpose, which is to bring students together in a spirit of collaborative learning on the art and science of investment. Most curricula don't directly deal with finance and investment as it applies to individuals, but rather deal with markets and policies. Important concepts, no doubt, but not always directly applicable on a personal level. Hands-on practice investing is critical to the development of any skill, and in the case of investing, it's also statistically remunerative, but admittedly only in the long-term. That's why it makes sense to learn early, and we believe college is the perfect time to start this journey. The Stanford Investment Club is a passionate group of Stanford undergraduates who seek to tackle this significant task, together heightening individual understanding of all forms of investment. "How should I invest right now and what do I need to know about money to keep my career and life running smoothly?" Throughout our meetings, we hope to help each of our members answer this question for themselves.
